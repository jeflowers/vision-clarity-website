Schedule Consultation
How long does the consultation take?

Your initial consultation will take approximately 90 minutes. This includes:

  • A comprehensive eye exam
  • Discussion of your vision correction options
  • Time to ask questions

Our goal is to provide you with all the information you need to make an informed decision.

What should I bring to my consultation?

Please bring:

  • Your current glasses or contact lens prescription
  • A list of medications you're taking
  • Your insurance card
  • Any medical records related to your eyes

Important: If you wear contact lenses, please remove them 48 hours prior to your appointment for soft contacts, or 3 weeks prior for rigid gas permeable lenses.

How should I prepare for my eye exam?

To prepare for your eye exam:

  • Get a good night's sleep before your appointment
  • Avoid alcohol for 24 hours before your exam
  • If you drive, bring sunglasses as your eyes may be dilated
  • Write down any questions or concerns you have

Procedures & Recovery

Am I a good candidate for LASIK?

Good candidates for LASIK typically:

  • Are at least 18 years old
  • Have stable vision for at least one year
  • Have adequate corneal thickness
  • Have no significant eye diseases
  • Are not pregnant or nursing

During your consultation, we'll perform comprehensive testing to determine if LASIK is right for you.

What is the recovery time after LASIK?

Most patients experience improved vision within 24-48 hours after LASIK surgery. You'll likely be able to return to work within 1-3 days.

We recommend avoiding strenuous activities for one week and swimming/hot tubs for two weeks. Your doctor will provide specific post-operative instructions during your consultation.

What alternatives to LASIK do you offer?

In addition to LASIK, we offer several vision correction alternatives including:

  • PRK (Photorefractive Keratectomy)
  • SMILE (Small Incision Lenticule Extraction)
  • Refractive Lens Exchange
  • Implantable Contact Lenses (ICL)

During your consultation, we'll discuss which procedure is best suited for your specific vision needs.
