What Is LASIK Eye Surgery?

Laser refractive surgery like LASIK corrects nearsightedness, farsightedness, and astigmatism by reshaping the cornea. Most patients may see better without glasses or contacts after a surgeon produces a tiny flap in the cornea, uses a laser to restructure the corneal tissue, then repositions the flap.

Who Can Get LASIK?

LASIK eye surgery has been proven to be safe and effective, but not everyone is a candidate. If you are healthy, have stable vision and healthy eyes — free from infections or conditions like glaucoma or cataracts — and if you are diagnosed with nearsightedness, farsightedness or astigmatism, you may be a candidate.

PRK Alternative

For patients who aren't good candidates for LASIK, PRK offers an excellent alternative. This procedure achieves similar visual outcomes but uses a different approach that's better suited for certain eye conditions like thin corneas or irregular corneal surfaces.

Benefits of LASIK

Most people who have had LASIK find they no longer need to rely on glasses or contacts. The surgery can benefit you, if you've been diagnosed with nearsightedness (myopia), astigmatism or farsightedness (hyperopia). There are many benefits to LASIK eye surgery, including:

  • Gentle, patient-centered approach surgery requiring no bandages or stitches, making recovery easy.
  • Improved vision, with many achieving 20/20 vision or better
  • Quick and minimally invasive procedure, usually taking only 10 minutes per eye
  • Easy recovery with improvements in vision starting as soon as hours to a few days following the procedure
  • Minimal discomfort with only a slight pressure on the eyes
  • Lasting results
  • Reduced allergy and infection risk, since there's no need for contact lenses after LASIK
