What Is LASIK Eye Surgery?

Our surgeons use special lasers to reshape your cornea, the primary lens of the eye and change its focusing power. This eye treatment corrects your vision, so you can reduce or eliminate your dependence on glasses and contact lenses.

Our ophthalmologists perform a complete eye exam to make sure you're a good candidate for LASIK. We explain your options thoroughly and answer all your questions.

Who Can Get LASIK?

LASIK eye surgery has been proven to be safe and effective, but not everyone is a candidate. If you are healthy, have stable vision and healthy eyes — free from infections or conditions like glaucoma or cataracts — and if you are diagnosed with nearsightedness, farsightedness or astigmatism, you may be a candidate.

PRK as an Alternative

Photorefractive keratectomy (PRK) is an excellent alternative for those who may not be ideal candidates for LASIK. PRK was the first laser vision correction procedure and continues to offer excellent outcomes for patients with thin corneas or other specific conditions.

Benefits of LASIK

The surgery is painless and requires no bandages or stitches, making recovery easy. Most people who have had LASIK find they no longer need to rely on glasses or contacts. The surgery can benefit you, if you've been diagnosed with nearsightedness (myopia), astigmatism or farsightedness (hyperopia). There are many benefits to LASIK eye surgery, including:

  • Improved vision, with many achieving 20/20 vision or better
  • Quick and minimally invasive procedure, usually taking only 10 minutes per eye
  • Easy recovery with improvements in vision starting as soon as hours to a few days following the procedure
  • Minimal discomfort with only a slight pressure on the eyes
  • Lasting results
  • Reduced allergy and infection risk, since there's no need for contact lenses after LASIK
