Diagnostic Technology

Precision mapping and analysis for personalized treatment plans

Wavefront Analyzer

Our advanced wavefront technology creates a detailed 3D map of your eye's unique optical system, measuring and identifying even the most subtle vision imperfections that traditional methods might miss. This allows for completely personalized treatments that address your specific visual needs.

Corneal Topographer

This sophisticated imaging system maps the exact shape and curvature of your cornea with microscopic precision. By analyzing the corneal surface, we can identify irregularities and ensure optimal treatment planning, especially important for patients with astigmatism or unusual corneal conditions.

Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT)

Our OCT system provides ultra-high resolution cross-sectional imaging of your cornea and retina. This non-invasive technology allows us to visualize the distinct layers of your eye to precisely measure corneal thickness and detect any abnormalities that might affect your treatment plan.

Laser Technology

Precision instruments for optimal vision correction

Femtosecond Laser

Used in our bladeless LASIK procedures, this ultra-fast laser creates precise corneal flaps with accuracy measured in microns. The femtosecond laser produces extremely short pulses of energy, allowing for incredibly precise tissue separation without generating heat, resulting in more consistent flap thickness and reduced complications.

Wavefront-Guided Excimer Laser

Our advanced excimer laser system reshapes your cornea with extreme precision to correct your vision. Integrated with wavefront technology, the laser treatment is customized to your unique visual profile. The system features high-speed eye tracking that adjusts for even the smallest eye movements during the procedure, ensuring perfect alignment.

Advanced Eye Tracking System

Our laser systems incorporate sophisticated eye-tracking technology that monitors your eye position more than 1,000 times per second. This ensures that the laser treatment remains perfectly centered even with microscopic eye movements, delivering consistent results and enhancing safety throughout the procedure.
