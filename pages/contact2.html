Frequently Asked Questions

Scheduling & Appointments

How do I schedule a consultation?

You can schedule a consultation by calling our office at (800) 555-2020, using our online scheduling tool on our website, or emailing us at info@eyecarecenter.com. Our staff will help find a time that works best for you.

How long does the consultation take?

Initial consultations typically take 60-90 minutes. This includes time for comprehensive eye testing, discussion of your vision goals, and answering any questions you may have about potential treatments.

What should I bring to my consultation?

Please bring your current glasses and/or contact lenses, a list of medications you're taking, your vision insurance information, and any previous eye exam records if available. Also, it's helpful to prepare a list of questions you may have about your vision and treatment options.

How should I prepare for my eye exam?

For your eye exam, we recommend getting plenty of rest the night before. If you wear contact lenses, please remove them at least 24 hours before your appointment for soft lenses, or several days for rigid lenses. Avoid applying eye makeup on the day of your exam, and bring sunglasses as your eyes may be sensitive after dilation.

Insurance & Payment

Do you accept insurance?

Yes, we accept most major vision and health insurance plans, including VSP, EyeMed, Blue Cross Blue Shield, and more. Please call our office with your insurance information so we can verify your coverage before your appointment.

Do you offer payment plans?

Yes, we offer flexible payment plans to help make your eye care affordable. We accept CareCredit and also have in-house financing options available with approved credit. Our staff can help you explore payment options that work for your budget.

What is the cost of LASIK surgery?

The cost of LASIK surgery typically ranges from $2,000 to $3,000 per eye, depending on the technology used and your specific vision needs. We provide comprehensive pricing during your consultation that includes pre-operative care, the procedure itself, and post-operative follow-up appointments.

Procedures & Recovery

Am I a good candidate for LASIK?

Good LASIK candidates are typically 18 years or older with stable vision for at least a year, have adequate corneal thickness, and do not have certain eye conditions like severe dry eye or glaucoma. During your consultation, we'll perform comprehensive testing to determine if LASIK is right for you or if another procedure might be more suitable.

What is the recovery time after LASIK?

Most patients experience improved vision within 24 hours after LASIK. You'll need someone to drive you home after the procedure, and you should plan to rest for the remainder of that day. Many patients return to work within 1-2 days, though you should avoid strenuous activities for about a week. Complete healing typically occurs within 3-6 months.

What alternatives to LASIK do you offer?

We offer several alternatives to LASIK, including PRK (Photorefractive Keratectomy), SMILE (Small Incision Lenticule Extraction), implantable contact lenses (ICL), and refractive lens exchange (RLE). During your consultation, we'll discuss which option might be best for your specific vision needs and eye health.
